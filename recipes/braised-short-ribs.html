Allium-Free Braised Short Ribs

These melt-in-your-mouth, red wine braised short ribs are deeply comforting and packed with robust flavors. By building an aromatic base of fennel, carrots, and celery, and reinforcing the broth with a touch of Hondashi and tomato paste, this recipe achieves a rich, complex, umami-packed sauce without a single allium.

Yield 4 - 6 servings
Prep Time 15 mins
Cook Time 1.5 - 3 hrs
💡
Flavor Swap: The Savory Backbone

Traditional braises rely on mountains of onions and garlic. Here, we build a sophisticated flavor foundation using extra carrots and celery for earthy depth, plus optional fennel for its delicate sweetness. A spoonful of tomato paste roasted in the pan, combined with a splash of Hondashi (dashi powder) and fish sauce, creates an incredibly rich, savory umami backbone that makes the sauce intensely flavorful and rewarding.

Ingredients

The Meat

  • ~8 pieces of bone-in short rib, 1.5 to 2 inches thick
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

The Aromatic Base

  • 1/2 medium fennel bulb, cored and thinly sliced (optional, or substitute with 1 extra celery rib)
  • 3 medium carrots, chopped
  • 3 ribs celery, chopped
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • A pinch dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves

The Braising Liquid

  • 1 cup Cabernet Sauvignon (or other dry red wine)
  • 1 cup water (or homemade, strictly allium-free beef/chicken stock)
  • 4 tbsp (1/4 cup) balsamic vinegar
  • 2 - 3 tbsp brown sugar (adjust to your sweetness preference)
  • 2 tbsp regular soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tsp Hondashi powder

The Thickener

  • 5 tbsp cornstarch
  • 6 tbsp cold water

Instructions

Part 1: The Aromatic Base

  1. Season the Short Ribs: Pro Tip: For the best flavor, generously season the short ribs on all sides with salt and pepper up to 24 hours in advance and leave them uncovered in the fridge. This deeply seasons the meat and guarantees a better crust. If you didn't do this ahead of time, season them generously right now.
  2. Heat the Pot: If using an Instant Pot, turn to the Sauté function (More/High). If oven braising, use a large heavy-bottomed Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Once the pot is hot, add 1 tbsp olive oil.
  3. Brown the Meat: Place the ribs into the pot and brown them deeply for 4 minutes per side. Work in batches to avoid overcrowding the pot (which will steam the meat instead of searing it). Once beautifully browned all over, set the ribs aside on a plate.
  4. Sauté the Aromatic Base: In the remaining beef fat and olive oil, add the chopped carrots, celery, and sliced fennel (if using). Sauté for 4 to 5 minutes until the vegetables begin to soften and become fragrant.
  5. Toast the Aromatics: Stir in the tomato paste, dried thyme, and bay leaves. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes until the tomato paste deepens to a dark brick-red color.
  6. Deglaze the Pot: Pour in the 1 cup of red wine. Use a wooden spoon to scrub all the beautiful, flavorful browned bits off the bottom of the pot. Let the wine simmer for about a minute to allow the alcohol to evaporate slightly.
  7. Build the Braising Liquid: Add the water (or allium-free stock), balsamic vinegar, brown sugar, soy sauce, fish sauce, and Hondashi powder to the pot. Stir to combine.
  8. Nestle the Meat: Carefully place the browned short ribs back into the pot, pouring in any resting juices from the plate.

Part 2: The Cook (Choose Your Method)

Method A: Instant Pot

  1. Pressure Cook: Secure the Instant Pot lid and ensure the venting knob is set to Sealing. Cook on High Pressure for 52 minutes. Once the cooking cycle completes, allow for a 15-minute Natural Release. Carefully turn the knob to Venting to release any remaining pressure before opening the lid.

Method B: Traditional Oven Braise

  1. Braise in the Oven: Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C). Ensure your Dutch oven's liquid comes about halfway up the sides of the meat (add a little extra stock or water if needed). Bring the liquid to a simmer on the stovetop, then cover with a tight-fitting lid and transfer to the preheated oven.
  2. Slow Cook: Braise for 2.5 to 3 hours, until the meat is incredibly tender and easily pulls away from the bone.

Part 3: The Finish

  1. Thicken the Sauce: Gently transfer the exquisitely tender short ribs to a serving platter. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and cold water to create a slurry. If using an Instant Pot, turn it back to Sauté. If using a Dutch oven, place it over medium heat on the stovetop. Once the sauce begins to simmer, slowly stream in the slurry, a little at a time, whisking constantly until the sauce reaches your desired glossy thickness. Taste and adjust seasoning with a pinch of salt or brown sugar if needed.
  2. Serve: Return the short ribs to the thickened red wine sauce just to coat them and keep them hot. Serve immediately. Enjoy the incredibly rich, melt-in-your-mouth texture and complex flavor!
