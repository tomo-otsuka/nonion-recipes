Taiwanese Oxtail Noodle Soup

A perfect orchestration of fall-off-the-bone oxtail, a rich and slightly spicy broth, fresh noodles, tender bok choy, and that absolutely necessary fistful of Chinese pickled mustard greens. This allium-free adaptation maintains all the deep, complex flavor of the traditional favorite.

Yield 6-8 servings
Prep Time 30 mins
Cook Time 2 hrs
💡
Flavor Swap: The Savory Backbone

Traditional Taiwanese Beef Noodle Soup relies heavily on scallions, garlic, and onions. To replicate this savory depth without alliums, we build a robust aromatic base using caramelized fennel, celery, and daikon radish, which provide essential earthy sweetness. We also increase the amount of fresh ginger for aromatic heat, and swap the scallion garnish for tender inner celery leaves. Note: Always check your doubanjiang (spicy bean paste) label, as some brands include garlic.

Ingredients

The Meat

  • 3 pounds oxtail (cut into segments)

The Aromatic Base

  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil
  • 3-inch piece fresh ginger (smashed)
  • 1 large fennel bulb (cut into wedges)
  • 2 stalks celery (cut into 2-inch segments)
  • 1 small daikon radish (peeled and cut into half-moons)
  • 1 tomato (cut into wedges)
  • 4 dried red chilies (ripped in half)

The Braising Liquid

  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons allium-free spicy bean paste (douban jiang) or plain fermented bean paste mixed with chili flakes
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup Shaoxing wine
  • 4 cups water (plus more to taste at the end)
  • 1 teaspoon Hondashi (optional, for an extra umami boost)
  • 1 Chinese aromatic herb packet (lu bao) OR homemade spice sachet (see below)

Homemade Spice Sachet (Optional)

  • 1 Chinese cinnamon stick
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1/2 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 tablespoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1/4 teaspoon whole black peppercorns

For Serving

  • 32 ounces fresh wheat noodles
  • Bok choy (a small handful per serving)
  • Fresh cilantro (finely chopped)
  • Tender inner celery leaves (finely chopped)
  • Chinese pickled mustard greens (suan cai / snow vegetable)

Instructions

Part 1: Blanch the Oxtail

  1. Boil the Meat: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Carefully add the oxtail pieces. Let the water return to a boil and cook for 2 to 3 minutes to draw out impurities.
  2. Rinse: Drain the oxtail in a colander and rinse each piece thoroughly under cold running water. Set aside.

Part 2: Build the Soup (Instant Pot Method)

  1. Sauté the Aromatics: Turn your Instant Pot to the Sauté setting. Add the oil, smashed ginger, fennel wedges, and celery. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until lightly caramelized and fragrant. Add the daikon radish, tomato wedges, and dried chilies, cooking for another 2 minutes.
  2. Add the Flavor Base: Return the blanched oxtail to the pot. Add the tomato paste, allium-free doubanjiang, sugar, soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine. Stir well to coat the meat and aromatics in the sauce.
  3. Simmer: Pour in the 4 cups of water and add the Hondashi (if using) along with your Chinese aromatic herb packet (or homemade cheesecloth spice sachet). Ensure the pot is not filled past the maximum safety line (halve the recipe if using a small pressure cooker).
  4. Pressure Cook: Cancel the Sauté function. Close the lid, ensure the venting valve is set to Sealing, and cook on the Meat/Stew (or High Pressure) setting for 100 minutes. (Stovetop alternative: Bring to a boil in a large Dutch oven, reduce to a low simmer, cover, and cook for 3 to 4 hours until the oxtail is fall-off-the-bone tender).
  5. Release Pressure: Once the timer is up, carefully perform a quick release (use a wooden spoon or wear a mitt to avoid the steam) or let the pressure release naturally for 20 minutes before opening the valve.

Part 3: Serve

  1. Cook the Noodles and Greens: While the soup finishes, boil a separate pot of water. Cook your fresh wheat noodles according to the package instructions. In the last 1-2 minutes of cooking, drop in the bok choy to blanch until just tender. Drain well.
  2. Adjust and Assemble: Taste the broth and add more hot water if the flavor is too concentrated (up to 4 additional cups). Divide the cooked noodles and bok choy among large soup bowls. Ladle the rich, hot oxtail broth over the noodles, ensuring everyone gets a few generous pieces of tender oxtail, daikon, and fennel.
  3. Garnish: Top each bowl generously with finely chopped cilantro, celery leaves, and a large spoonful of Chinese pickled mustard greens. Serve immediately.
